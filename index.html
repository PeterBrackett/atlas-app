Opportunity scorecard — Overall by segment

Auto-score "Market opportunity" from an allocation: Scores every segment in every loaded country at once, based on the asset class picked here (independent of the "Show:" selector below): below threshold = 1, at or above threshold = 2, at or above optimal = 3. A segment with no allocation data for this selection is treated as $0 (scores 1, unless threshold is 0 or less). Overwrites any existing Market opportunity score. This panel is always here whenever you're signed in — it no longer depends on which view is selected below.
Auto-score "Regulatory complexity" from TMF Group rankings (): TMF's rank 1 is the most complex jurisdiction to do business in, rank 79 the least — this is inverted from the allocation scorer above since low complexity is the attractive case: rank at or below threshold = 1, above threshold = 2, at or above optimal = 3. Countries TMF doesn't cover (Iceland, as of the 2025 edition) are skipped and left as-is — reported after applying. Same segment-independent country-level value applied to every segment, and overwrites any existing Regulatory complexity score.
Auto-score "Distribution resources required" from institution concentration: Uses each segment's top-3-institution share of AUM (from the same Company-file institution data segment totals are built from) as a proxy for how much distribution resource covering the market would take: below threshold = 1, at or above threshold = 2, at or above optimal = 3 — a handful of large institutions dominating a segment needs less coverage effort than AUM spread across thousands of small ones. Segments with no institution-level concentration data (e.g. Life/Non-life insurance, built from industry aggregates rather than individual institutions) are skipped, not guessed at. Overwrites any existing Distribution resources required score.
Auto-score "Languages required" from EF English Proficiency Index (): EF EPI score runs 0-800, higher = more of the population speaks English well — direction matches the allocation scorer above (not inverted): below threshold = 1, at or above threshold = 2, at or above optimal = 3. The US, UK, Ireland, Canada, Australia and New Zealand aren't ranked by EF EPI at all (it only measures non-native proficiency), so they're automatically scored 3 — English is the native language there by definition. Singapore is also unranked by EF EPI but is scored 3 as a judgment call, since English is Singapore's dominant language of government, courts and business; override this per-segment if you'd rather treat it differently. Iceland has no EF EPI coverage and isn't an English-native/business market, so it's genuinely uncovered and skipped, same as Iceland's Regulatory complexity gap. Same country-level value applied to every segment, and overwrites any existing Languages required score.
